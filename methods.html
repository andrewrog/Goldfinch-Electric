Methods

Splicing

Splices are all done using uninsulated crimp style connections and adhesive heat shrink or soldering and adhesive heat shrink depending on the area that the splice is in. In high heat/high vibration areas we use crimp style connections. In lower heat/lower vibration areas we use soldering to make good long-lasting electrical connections.

Fabrication

Mounts for lights and other equipment we can make with through steel and finished with primer and paint. Our current welding process is flux core. We can also 3D print custom bezels, switch plates, clamps, grommets, and more to make finish and fitment as OEM as possible. Our 3D modelling is done with the help of Autodesk Fusion 360 and our 3D printing is done with the help of a Bambu Labs P1S printer. Filament types that we use include ABS, ASA, PETG, and PLA. Our go to filament type for parts that require higher strength is ABS.

Diagnostics

If something is not working right after an installation or you feel that something is not quite right with the electrical system on your vehicle call to . Some of our testing equipment includes: Multimeter, battery tester and a borescope

Protection

We use tesa tape in areas where our wiring is exposed to high heat environments. Traditional split loom or braided sleeving where deemed appropriate.
